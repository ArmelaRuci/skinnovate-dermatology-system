Skinnovate

AI-powered dermatology, end to end

A full-stack clinic platform where patients upload skin photos, AI diagnoses the condition, and dermatologists validate and prescribe — all in one connected workflow.

React 18 Flask 3 TensorFlow / Keras PostgreSQL 16 JWT Auth Docker

Platform flow

Patient
Uploads skin photo
AI Model
Predicts condition + confidence
Doctor
Reviews & validates diagnosis
Doctor
Creates treatment plan
Patient
Tracks progress & books follow-ups

Patient

Role

01

Patient Dashboard

A personalised health summary that brings together every dimension of the patient's activity — AI analyses, appointments, treatments, and loyalty points — into one glanceable view.

Four stat cards: total AI analyses, scheduled appointments, active treatments, and accumulated loyalty points (earned +5 per analysis)
Recent Analyses panel shows the latest AI results with condition name, confidence score, severity badge, and doctor review status
Upcoming Appointments panel surfaces the next scheduled visit with date, time, and type
Active Treatment Plans listed at the bottom — quick glance at what's currently prescribed
Connects to → Every card links into its dedicated section; analyses shown here are the same records the doctor reviews in the validation queue
Dashboard

02

AI Skin Analysis

The core feature of the platform. The patient uploads a photo of the affected skin area and the model — trained on four classes (Acne, Eczema, Milia, Rosacea) — returns an instant preliminary diagnosis.

Drag-and-drop upload zone accepts JPG, PNG, and WebP up to 16 MB
Image is preprocessed server-side through the exact pipeline used during training: resize to 224×224, unsharp mask, CLAHE on the L channel, normalise
Returns predicted condition, confidence score, severity level, and a flag for doctor consultation if confidence is below 70%
Full analysis history shown on the right: thumbnail, condition, confidence, severity badge, and review status for every previous upload
Connects to → Each submitted analysis lands directly in the doctor's Pending Reviews queue, awaiting validation
AI Analysis

03

Appointments

Full appointment lifecycle management. Patients book visits with available dermatologists and track them through every status transition from request to completion.

Filter bar segments appointments by All, Scheduled, Requested, Completed, and Cancelled
Each appointment card shows date, time, type (In Person / Video), current status badge, and an Emergency flag when urgency is flagged
Cancel action available directly on the card for appointments that haven't started
Book Appointment button opens the scheduling flow, showing only doctors with available slots
Connects to → Booked appointments appear in the doctor's Schedule view; emergency-flagged ones are surfaced with an Urgent badge
Appointments

04

Health Records

A read-only view of the patient's complete medical history. Everything written by dermatologists — consultation notes and prescriptions — is stored here and accessible at any time.

Two tabbed sections: Medical Notes (written by the dermatologist after each visit) and Prescriptions (e-prescriptions issued through the platform)
Each note shows the date and the full consultation text written by the doctor
Record count in each tab label keeps the patient informed of how much history exists
Connects to → Records are created by the doctor from the Review Diagnosis page — Medical Note and Treatment Plan panels write directly into this view
Records

05

Treatment Plans

Patients track their ongoing and completed dermatology treatments in one place. Each plan is created by the doctor and reflects the prescribed approach for the diagnosed condition.

Filter between Active, Completed, and All treatment plans
Each card shows treatment name, type (Topical, Oral, Procedural), status badge, and start date
Progress logging allows doctors to record milestones and observations over the treatment timeline
Connects to → Treatment plans are created by the doctor from the Treatment Plan panel on the Review Diagnosis page, directly linked to the AI diagnosis record
Treatments

Dermatologist

Role

06

Doctor Dashboard

The clinical command centre. The doctor sees at a glance how many AI analyses are waiting for their review, what's on today's schedule, and their overall validation track record.

Four stat cards: Pending Reviews, Today's Appointments, Total Validated, and Upcoming — all live from the database
Pending Reviews panel lists every unvalidated AI diagnosis with condition, date, confidence score, severity, and a direct Review button
Low-confidence cases (below 70%) show a "Consultation recommended" warning, prompting the doctor to prioritise them
Today's Appointments panel shows the patient name, appointment type, time, and emergency flag for the current day's schedule
Connects to → Clicking Review on any pending case opens the full Review Diagnosis page; Today's Appointments link to the Schedule view
Doctor Dashboard

07

Review Diagnosis

The most clinically important page in the platform. The doctor reviews the patient's uploaded image alongside the AI's prediction, then validates, modifies, adds notes, and prescribes a treatment — all in one screen.

Patient Information panel: full name, email, and patient ID for identity confirmation
The original uploaded image is displayed at full resolution alongside the upload timestamp
AI Diagnosis panel: primary condition, confidence score, severity badge, and a full breakdown bar chart of all four class scores
Confirm AI Diagnosis — accepts the model's output as the official record; Modify Diagnosis — lets the doctor override the condition entirely
Medical Note panel: free-text consultation notes saved directly to the patient's Health Records
Treatment Plan panel: creates a named treatment plan of a specified type, linked to this diagnosis record
Connects to → Confirming or modifying updates the diagnosis status seen by the patient ("Awaiting doctor review" → validated); Medical Note and Treatment Plan write to the patient's Records and Treatments pages respectively
Review Diagnosis

08

My Schedule

A chronological view of all upcoming appointments, grouped by date. The doctor manages their clinical day from here — starting consultations and marking them as done in real time.

Appointments grouped by date with a count badge per day — no calendar clutter, pure list clarity
Each card shows exact time, patient name, appointment type, status badge, and urgency level (Urgent / Emergency)
Start and Done action buttons update the appointment state machine: Scheduled → In Progress → Completed
Connects to → Appointment status changes here are reflected immediately in the patient's Appointments page — the same record, seen from both sides
